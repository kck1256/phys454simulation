Retrograde Motion


by Kyra Koe

Click anywhere to begin.... Then keep clicking!

The yellow circle in the middle represents the Sun.
The blue circle represents the Earth.
The red circle represents any other planet, say Mars.
Each successive blue or red circle represents the revolution of the planet around the sun.

The elongation of the white line that connects the two planets represents the apparent movement toward the left as a person on Earth observes Mars.
After the 7th, you'll notice that the line gets shorter. This means that Mars appears to move towards the right of the sky as compared to 24 hours prior.

However, around the 11th click, you'll notice that the line starts to get longer again. This means that Mars will appear to move towards the left of the sky again.
